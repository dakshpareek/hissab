This case became a flashpoint for the broader debate about systemic accountability. The restaurant's illegal operation required multiple failures across multiple agencies — the beat officer who patrols the area, the MCD officer who issues permissions, and the licence-signing official. Yet only the restaurant owner faces legal consequences.

This pattern — private actors prosecuted, government enablers untouched — is precisely what Hissab tracks.
