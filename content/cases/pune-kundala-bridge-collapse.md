The Kundala bridge collapse is a recurring type in India: an infrastructure project where multiple checkpoints — material quality, structural design, site supervision — all failed simultaneously, yet accountability is diffused across agencies until it disappears entirely.

The FIR that names people but leads to no arrests is one of the most common patterns in these cases. Without judicial prodding or media persistence, such FIRs often die in the investigation stage.
