The Kundmala bridge collapse became a public-record accountability case because the immediate tragedy was only the first layer of the story. The later record centered on maintenance responsibility, administrative delay, and the question of how a widely used footbridge remained unsafe long enough to fail in the first place.

The incident was not a case of a named accused being arrested in the public record. Instead, the follow-up reporting and official statements pointed toward a probe, inter-department responsibility disputes, and suspension of PWD officials after the collapse.
